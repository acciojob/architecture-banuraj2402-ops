Classical vs. Modern Architecture

Classical Architecture, exemplified by ancient Greek and Roman temples, focuses on symmetry, proportion, and geometry. Key elements include columns (Doric, Ionic, Corinthian), pediments, and friezes. These designs were meant to convey order and permanence. The golden ratio often influenced proportions to achieve aesthetic harmony.

Modern Architecture, emerging in the early 20th century, rejected ornamentation and embraced functionalism. It utilizes materials like steel, concrete, and glass, prioritizing space and minimalism. Architects like Le Corbusier and Mies van der Rohe were central to this movement, defining the clean lines of the contemporary urban landscape.
